4. Case Study Analyses: Harassment by Online Lending Apps in the Philippines

4.1 Summary of the Incident

On January 31, 2025, NBI and PAOCC raided online lending offices due to reports of harassment and death threats made to borrowers. The apps accessed contacts and shamed borrowers publicly. Victims faced mental health issues, including suicide attempts. Violations include RA 10173, RA 10175, and unethical collection practices.

4.2 Ethical Issues Involved

Victims of online lending apps faced severe psychological harm from constant threats and harassment. The apps violated privacy laws by misusing borrower contacts without consent and failed to inform users about data collection. Many were publicly shamed with false accusations, while deceptive loan tactics trapped users in fear and abuse.

4.3 Application of Ethical Theories

These lending apps ignored their ethical duty to treat clients with respect and honesty, using threats, coercion, and violating privacy, which are morally wrong regardless of outcomes. Their behavior lacked virtues like integrity, empathy, and fairness. Even if debts were recovered, the emotional trauma and reputational harm far outweigh the benefits, making the actions unethical.

4.4 Ethical Solutions & Interventions

  • Enforce RA 10173 for transparent and ethical data use.
  • Launch public awareness on unethical lending and data safety.
  • Tighten regulations on app permissions and collection tactics.
  • Provide mental health support for harassment victims.
  • Promote ethical fintech practices prioritizing trust and transparency.
  • Prosecute violators under RA 10175 and RA 10173.
